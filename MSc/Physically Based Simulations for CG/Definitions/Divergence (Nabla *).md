Divergence "$\nabla \cdot$"
## Definition
$$
\begin{align}
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} &= \left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x}, \frac{\partial}{\partial y}, \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \right)^{T} \mathbf{u} \\
&= \frac{\partial \mathbf{u_{x}}}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial \mathbf{u_{y}}}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial \mathbf{u_{z}}}{\partial z}
\end{align}
$$
Note:
- For $u : \mathbb{R}^{n}$, we have $\nabla \cdot u \in \mathbb{R}$.
- Corresponds to $\nabla \cdot u = tr(\nabla \mathbf{u})$.
